Where this sits
existence → instantiation

Caro et al. (2024) prove that an honest BQP prover and a BPP verifier exist for the Fourier-sparse learning problem; an asymptotic statement. They provide a protocol for doing so, but not the implementation.

This project supplies the missing artefact. Two Python packages, mos/ (state-level emulator) and ql/ (Thm. 12 interactive pair), with two equivalent backends, ~30k trials across seven experiments, and protobuf-aggregated results.

08 / 09 Limitations & contributions

Empirical mechanism study, not an asymptotic validation. Two failure modes characterised; one open question raised.

Limitations
own these before they’re asked
  • Sample budgets three orders below analytic Hoeffding. For |L|=10, ε=0.3, δ=0.1 the requirement is ≈ 3.8×10⁷; experiments use ~10⁴. Maps mechanisms, not asymptotic complexity.
  • No real quantum hardware. Every measurement is classical simulation. Cross-talk, decay, biased readout are unstressed.
  • Brute-force circuit oracle caps gate-noise study at n=8. The MCX construction is exponential; the Statevector path avoids this but cannot carry per-gate noise.
Contributions
what this report adds
  • First faithful implementation of the MoS protocol
  • Two failure modes characterised. Vanishing-margin acceptance ceiling under mixed-coefficient cheats; prover-side cliff under per-gate depolarising noise.
  • One open theoretical question raised. Per-gate depolarising noise: Ma–Su–Deng treat output-side, not gate-side.

09 / 09 FUTURE WORK

Three directions in roughly decreasing impact on what this report can support.

01 · sample budgets

Analytic-budget validation.

Re-run soundness at the full Hoeffding budget. Upgrades present empirical agreement from a “good-enough” state to an exact model.

02 · open question

Formal treatment of per-gate noise.

Extend Ma–Su–Deng's output-side bounds (measurement noise, end-of-circuit depolarisation) to per-gate depolarisation inside the QFS circuit, and derive a closed-form (n, p) verifiability boundary that predicts the empirical cliff at n ≥ 6.

03 · hardware

Real quantum hardware.

Any IBM backend with effective per-gate error below 10⁻³ should admit honest acceptance at n=6: a concrete success criterion from the cliff.
